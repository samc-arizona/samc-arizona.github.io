English Artifacts

Throughout this class, I produced many written assignments, and doing so helped me grow and learn as a writer. The assignments also serve as artifacts which can help to illustrate this growth, acting as snapshots of specific points in my writing journey this year.

Major Artifacts

Literacy Narrative

The Literacy Narrative was the first major essay I wrote for this class. As the first, I think it was the easiest essay I had to write, mostly because it was very personal, so I didn't really have to do any research or work hard to find the material I wrote about. However, this lack of research allowed and forced me to focus on the other parts of essay writing much more, especially thinking about its rhetorical situation. While working on this essay I also focused more on revising larger scale things like structure based on actionable feedback from my peers, as opposed to just getting feedback on grammar and clarity of specific sentences.

Essay 2: Research Proposal

The research proposal, while not an essay, was the next major assignment. This assignment took me in an almost opposite direction from the Literacy Narrative; it was entirely focused on finding and evaluating evidence, with almost no writing. Because of this, by working on and producing this proposal I got lots of practice finding evidence and evaulating sources based on quality and especially relevance.

When I started working on this assignment, I was okay at searching for sources, but I still was doing it inefficiently and in a shallow manner. As we continued work on this in class and I became more familiar with my case and what I wanted for the final essay, I started to employ better search strategies, including more effective keyword use in the library search and date range limits in general google searches. This helped immensely in quickly finding sources, especially as the deadlines approached.

Essay 2: Court Case Rhetorical Analysis

The rhetorical analysis of media coverage of a court case was the largest and last major essay I wrote for this class. This major assignment, unlike the previous two, did not have a facet absent or missing and required full focus on all the important parts of writing an essay. Through writing this essay I improved my ability to analyze not only the rhetorical situation of what I am writing, but also that of other works. Coming into class I had definitely thought about the audience and purpose of other texts, but I had never really found textual evidence to support that, like I somewhat did in this essay.

This essay really required me to think and write both with evidence and about the rhetorical situation. These aspects of writing were those the previous two assignments focused on. In this way essay 2 itself was a kind of synthesis of the different parts of writing I had practiced previously.
