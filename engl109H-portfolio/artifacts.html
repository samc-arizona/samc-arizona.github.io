English Artifacts

Throughout this class, I produced many written assignments, and doing so helped me grow and learn as a writer. The assignments also serve as artifacts which can help to illustrate this growth, acting as snapshots of specific points in my writing journey this year.

Major Artifacts

Literacy Narrative

The Literacy Narrative was the first major essay I wrote for this class. As the first, I think it was the easiest essay I had to write, mostly because it was very personal, so I didn't really have to do any research or work hard to find the material I wrote about. However, this lack of research allowed and forced me to focus on the other parts of essay writing much more, especially thinking about its rhetorical situation. While working on this essay I also focused more on revising larger scale things like structure based on actionable feedback from my peers, as opposed to just getting feedback on grammar and clarity of specific sentences.

Essay 2: Research Proposal

The Research proposal, while not an essay, was the next major assignment. This assignment took me in an almost opposite direction from the Literacy Narrative; it was entirely focused on finding and evaluating evidence, with almost no writing. Because of this, by working on and producing this proposal I got lots of practice finding evidence and evaulating sources based on quality and especially relevance.

When I started working on this assignment, I was ok at searching for sources, but I still was doing it inefficiently and in a shallow manner. As we continued work on this in class and I became more familiar with my case and what I wanted for the final essay, I started to employ better search strategies, including more effective keyword use in the library search and date range limits in general google searches. This helped immensely in quickly finding sources, especially as the deadlines approached.
