Honest Self Assesment

Overall

Throughout this semester I think I have done just okay in this class. While I have written enough for most of the assignments and felt the quality of my writing improve over time, I have also struggled plenty with time management. This has probably been one of my biggest weaknesses as a writer; it often takes me an inordinate amount of time to write the first paragraph/section/page.

In Class

Regarding in-class work and time, I think I have done pretty well. While I still struggled with writing quickly in-class, especially with the opening writes, as the semester went on I think my ability to put aside questions of quality and just put words on a page improved. Being physically in class also helped with this. In addition to individual writing in class, we also did peer activities, like the multiple peer reviews. While my slow writing originally plagued my peer reviews as well, by the end of the semester I think I was much better at quickly formalizing my feedback and providing in in a written form as well as audibly.

Out of Class

Out of class work was where I think I did the worst in this class. My main struggle time management. More specifically, I often started work too late to give myself adequate time for all the steps, and then missed out on or shrunk important parts of the writing process like revision. A lot of this struggle was caused by my perfectionism; I did not start soon enough because starting entailed writing some words and I did not feel what I was thinking of writing was up to my standards for writing assignments. This specific part of my problem with time management is also where I think this class has helped me the most. While the continuous flow of weekly assignments and emphasis on revision often felt like a lot or like it was causing me to fall behind, this constant flood of writing and focus on revision helped to hammer into my brain that it is in almost all cases better to write with very poor quality quickly than to write a bit better but with 1/10th the speed. The frequent revision assignments also helped with this, as they showed my subconsious brain time and time again that poor writing is not permanent and can be greatly improved without completely re-writing it.
