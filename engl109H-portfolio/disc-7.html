SLO 1: The Rhetorical Situation

In this class I have spent a much bigger part of my writing considering the audience(s) I am writing for that previously. While coming into this class I was pretty comfortable thinking about the rhetorical situation for things I am reading (the intended audience, authors, purpose), I was not used to really digging into the text to find evidence of audience and the rhetorical situation. Additionally, prior to the class I would think about my audience when I am writing very little, usually only if it was very clear, like I was writing an argument to a few specific people.

SLO 2: Research & Arguments

Overall, I think I came into this class with pretty solid foundations regarding using and synthesizing evidence. During this class however I had many opportunities to practice these skills, especially in the second unit. The main Essay 2 which I wrote serves as an example of how I have practiced and maintained my skill in use and synthesis of evidence. For the research aspect of this SLO, while I didn’t come in with no skill in research at all, I was still researching on a slightly more amateur level, mainly just using google and simple searches. Throughout the class and especially in the research phase of essay 2 and the essay 2 research major assignment I gained experience both using more specific and powerful searching strategies (using resources like library searches and more advanced keywords) and casting a wider net for supporting sources and bringing in a more varied and balanced array of evidence.

SLO 3: Genre Conventions & Language Mechanics

Throughout this class I have definitely grown in my proper use of language mechanics like grammar. This aspect of writing (learning the proper genre conventions and refining my local/minute use of language (grammar/mechanics)) has been one of my favorite to work on/improve in this class. The work I did for the top 20 revised essay 2 especially helped me grow in this aspect. While I did know and was proficient with most grammar before this assignment, I had not (in the past few years) really refreshed my knowledge of specific grammar rules (especially those regarding punctuation), and reading through the textbook for this revision assignment really helped to solidify in my mind the things I already knew, and point out where my knowledge/practice was lacking (mostly regarding use of punctuation like commas or dashes)

SLO 4: Revision & Peer Review

Starting out for this semester I was not at all used to reviewing and revising my own work, and only a little bit better at reading others work and giving them feedback. Now after the most of the semester is over I have gotten much more comfortable at revising my work, after being explicitly required to do so. This comes from two paths, just through repetition/practice I have gotten better, and through having revision required as a separate step. While the basic practice was important in improving my revision, having it required was much more valuable. Coming in, I almost never revised because I would either not give myself enough time (because I spent too long trying to write it perfect the first time), or due to aversion to reading my writing (because I think it is very bad, as I wrote it quickly to just have something done). However, after being required to re-read my work and revise it multiple times I have seen and experienced how important and useful revision can be. Additionally I began to actually enjoy revision, through the essay 1 clean/messy copy revision and especially the top 20 revision assignment where I carefully went through my writing addressing my most common mistakes.

SLO 5: Reflection & My Writing

This learning objective is one of the ones I think I may have progressed the most in, while at the same time being the one I put the least work into. This is most likely because at the beginning of the semester (and before this class in general), my writing journey and connection to language was not something I had considered/thought/written about at all. Through this class (and especially through the literacy narrative & some of the opening writes) I really began to think about my current and past relationship/experiences with language and writing, and how that affected both how I write and some of the reasons why I might write (outside of specific class assignments).
