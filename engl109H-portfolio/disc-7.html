2.1. SLO 1: The Rhetorical Situation

In this class I have spent a much bigger part of my writing considering the audience(s) I am writing for that previously. While coming into this class I was pretty comfortable thinking about the rhetorical situation for things I am reading (the intended audience, authors, purpose), I was not used to really digging into the text to find evidence of audience and the rhetorical situation. Additionally, prior to the class I would think about my audience when I am writing very little, usually only if it was very clear, like I was writing an argument to a few specific people.

2.4. SLO 4: Revision & Peer Review

Starting out for this semester I was not at all used to reviewing and revising my own work, and only a little bit better at reading others work and giving them feedback. Now after the most of the semester is over I have gotten much more comfortable at revising my work, after being explicitly required to do so. This comes from two paths, just through repetition/practice I have gotten better, and through having revision required as a separate step. While the basic practice was important in improving my revision, having it required was much more valuable. Coming in, I almost never revised because I would either not give myself enough time (because I spent too long trying to write it perfect the first time), or due to aversion to reading my writing (because I think it is very bad, as I wrote it quickly to just have something done).
